# [SRC-033]: Nashville Number System for Guitar — Capo and Sounding Key

**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / guitar blog

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "The Nashville Number System is not advanced secret-studio code. It is a practical shorthand for saying things like 'this song is a 1 - 5 - 6m - 4 progression' instead of locking the idea to one specific key forever."

> "Capo changes the sounding key. The number system helps you track the real progression instead of getting trapped in shape names."

> "If you play a 1 - 5 - 6m - 4 progression in G with a capo on the 2nd fret, the sounding chords become: A - E - F#m - D — but now it is in A major, not G major."

> "If you are talking to other musicians, making a chart, or trying to understand the real key, use the sounding key and the real number function instead of pretending the capo did nothing."

> "So if someone says a progression is I - V - vi - IV, and someone else writes 1 - 5 - 6m - 4, they are pointing at basically the same harmonic idea. The Nashville version is just the more chart-friendly version a lot of guitarists find easier to read quickly."

> "Move the progression to a second key. Keep the numbers the same and change only the chord names. If you use a capo, name the sounding key honestly. Do not hide behind the old shapes forever."

## Primary claims

- NNS descreve função harmônica, não formas de dedo — crítico quando capo altera o tom real.
- Capo + shapes familiares exige distinguir "tom das formas" vs "tom soando" (sounding key).
- NNS e análise romana (I–V–vi–IV) são equivalentes funcionalmente; NNS é shorthand de palco.
- Workflow prático: dizer números em voz alta, depois nomes de acordes; repetir em segundo tom.
- Sistema reduz adivinhação em mudanças de tom para cantor, capo ou shapes mais confortáveis.
