# [SRC-031]: Everything You Need to Know About the Nashville Number System

**URL**: https://www.premierguitar.com/lessons/chords/nashville-number-system
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / professional musician lesson (Annie Clements, Nashville bassist)

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "In the number system, we're simply eliminating the letter names of the notes and changing them to numbers instead."

> "When you get back to C, sing the number 1 again. Remember Julie Andrews in the Sound of Music? 'That will bring us back to Do!' From henceforth, C will be known as the number 1, as long as you're in the key of C, that is."

> "It's implied that when you see a 1 on the chart it will be a major chord, 2 will be minor, and so on. This follows the pattern we established above, when harmonizing the C major scale using triads: 1 major, 2 minor, 3 minor, 4 major, 5 major, 6 minor, 7 diminished."

> "It doesn't matter what key your song is in—this chart will work. This is the whole point of the number system. If someone wants to change the song's key, all you have to do is write the new key at the top of your chart. Nothing else on your chart has to change."

> "On a letter chart you would have to change every chord. Literally sit there and cross out every chord and write new letters in its place. No bueno. On a number chart, just cross out that 'C' and scribble in the new song key."

> "If your song is in a 'minor key,' you wouldn't kick off your chart with a 1-. That would completely negate the inherent chord values of the number system... Your song would simply start on the 6-. Same pool, different diving board."

## Primary claims

- NNS substitui nomes de notas por graus da escala maior (1–7), com qualidades implícitas derivadas da harmonização diatônica.
- Um único chart numérico serve para qualquer tom; mudança de tonalidade = alterar apenas o cabeçalho da tonalidade.
- Em "tom menor", o chart continua baseado na escala maior relativa; a progressão começa em 6- (não 1-).
- Notação inclui convenções de compasso (split bars, tick marks, diamonds, arrows) para uso em palco.
- Sistema usado profissionalmente em Nashville para chartar e transpor em tempo real sem instrumento.

## Relevance to charter Q3

Referência Tier A para o núcleo do NNS: transposição instantânea por função harmônica, contraste explícito com charts de letras (cifra), e regra de "minor key" como ponto de partida no 6- — essencial para mapear NNS vs graus romanos vs cifra brasileira.
