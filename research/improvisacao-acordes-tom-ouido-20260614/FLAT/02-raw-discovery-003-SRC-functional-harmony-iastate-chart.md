## Verbatim snippets / quotes
> Functional harmony in tonal music centers around the idea that tonic serves as the "home base" in a key and chords built on each scale degree in the scale have predictable relationships with each other and predictable movements as they ultimately progress towards tonic.

> Tonic is the ultimate goal in tonal music. It is home base in a key, and has the most stability and flexibility. Tonic can move to any chord.

> Chords in the dominant category most often directly precede tonic. Dominant function chords include V and vii˚.

> The predominant category comes before the dominant category on its way to tonic. The two chords contained in the predominant category are IV and ii.

> The first arrow that we can add to the chart is from V to vi, which is called deceptive motion because the vi chord acts as a temporary substitute for I.

> There are five types of motion possible between chords: Progression (P), Tonic (T), Retrogression (R), Similar Motion/Repetition (S), Elision (E).

## Primary claims (one-liners)
- Functional harmony chart orders diatonic chords: iii→vi→(IV,ii)→(V,vii°)→I with tonic most flexible.
- Deceptive (V→vi) and plagal (IV→I) motions are explicit exceptions on the chart.
- Five motion types (P/T/R/S/E) label how accompanists can classify chord-to-chord moves in real time.
- Expected vs unexpected progressions determine whether listeners can identify tonic by ear.

## Relevance to charter Q1
Formal left-to-right functional chart with motion taxonomy—foundation for building a predictive model of chord sequences and recognizing cadence types during accompaniment.
