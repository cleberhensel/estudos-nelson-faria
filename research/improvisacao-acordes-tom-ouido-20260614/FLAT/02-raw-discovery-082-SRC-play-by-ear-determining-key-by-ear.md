## Citações / trechos

> "Find the tonic: Pause the music and sing the note which feels like 'home', then find it on an instrument." — Sonofield

> "Most people try to find the key by guessing, or by relying on rules like 'it's the first chord' or 'it's the last chord.' Sometimes these work but often they don't." — Sonofield

> "Test the third: Play the minor third above the tonic. Then play the major third above the tonic and compare. Which fits the music better most of the time?" — Sonofield

> "Sing up and down that scale until you hit the one that sounds 'final'. That should be the tonal centre." — Music StackExchange

> "'Walk' down the scale step by step until you feel like you've 'arrived' on tonic. Scale degree 5/sol and scale degree 1/do are often confused." — UEN Pressbooks (Gary Karpinski)

> "With something so complex, it's nice if we can rely on and refine our intuition rather than ponder each of these factors one by one." — UEN Pressbooks

## Claims extraídos

1. Procedimento em 2 passos: (a) encontrar tônica; (b) determinar maior vs menor.
2. **Armadilhas**: assumir primeiro/último acorde como tom; confundir V (sol) com I (dó).
3. Teste da terça (M3 vs m3 sobre tônica) confirma tonalidade quando "feel" é ambíguo.
4. Método Karpinski: cantar nota proeminente → descer escala até sensação de repouso.
5. Treino recomendado: excertos simples (músicas infantis), identificar função (não nome absoluto) primeiro.
6. Wikipedia audio files com tom indicado como banco de exercícios verificáveis.
