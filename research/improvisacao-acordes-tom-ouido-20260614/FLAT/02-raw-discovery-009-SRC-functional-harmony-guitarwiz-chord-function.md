## Verbatim snippets / quotes
> Every chord in a key falls into one of three functional categories based on how it relates to the tonic (home) chord. These categories describe the chord's psychological and emotional role in the progression.

> When you play a V or V7 chord, you create harmonic tension. The listener's ear anticipates resolution back to the I chord. This tension-and-release cycle is one of the most fundamental satisfactions in music.

> A typical full progression creates a complete journey: I (home) - IV (departure) - V (tension) - I (resolution)

> Understanding function lets you make intelligent chord substitutions. Chords with the same function can often replace each other.

> When you're soloing over a C - F - G progression: Over C, you're at tonic - land on stable notes. Over F, you're moving forward. Over G, you're creating tension - the solo should acknowledge the dominant pull. Returning to C, resolve the tension.

## Primary claims (one-liners)
- I/iii/vi share tonic function; ii/IV share subdominant; V/vii° share dominant (with graded strength).
- I-IV-V-I is the archetypal functional journey for predicting harmonic direction.
- Same-function substitution (e.g., ii for IV, vi for I) preserves progression logic while changing color.
- Improvisation should track functional role per chord, not just scale patterns.

Guitar-specific application of functional prediction for accompaniment and improvisation—maps emotional/structural roles to fretboard decision-making when hearing or guessing next chords.
