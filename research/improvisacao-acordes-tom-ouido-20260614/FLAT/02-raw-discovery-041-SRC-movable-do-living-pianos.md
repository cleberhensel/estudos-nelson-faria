# [SRC-041]: Movable Do vs Fixed Do Solfeggio

**URL**: https://www.livingpianos.com/articles/which-is-better-movable-or-fixed-do-solfeggio/
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / ear training pedagogy

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "The notes in movable-do are based on pitch relationships so that 'do' is the tonic of the major key you are in. So, in a piece with no sharps or flats, C is 'do'. In a piece that has one sharp, G becomes 'do' because you may be in G major."

> "Movable do can be extremely valuable for developing your ear. It enables you to hear all intervals since all scales have the same pitch relationships."

> "If you want to be able to hear music and develop the ability to sight-sing music, transpose at sight, and transcribe music, learning movable-do solfege is an extremely valuable tool in achieving this."

> "Fixed-do solfege is a valuable tool in learning how to read a conductor score filled with various transpositions and a variety of clefs and being able to know what the absolute pitches are."

> "With Fixed-do, C is always 'do', D is always 're', E is always 'mi' and so on through the scale. You don't account for flats or sharps. So, it is basically note naming."

## Primary claims

- Dó móvel: "do" = tônica da tonalidade atual; relações intervalares constantes entre sílabas.
- Dó móvel favorece transposição à vista, ditado e percepção funcional.
- Dó fixo: sílabas mapeadas a pitch absoluto (C=do sempre); útil para leitura de partituras com transposições instrumentais.
- Dó móvel ≈ pensar em funções/graus; dó fixo ≈ pensar em nomes de notas.
- Debate pedagógico: cada sistema otimiza objetivos diferentes (relativo vs absoluto).

## Relevance to charter Q3

Paralelo vocal/teórico ao NNS e graus romanos: dó móvel internaliza a mesma lógica de "números/funções" vs "letras/pitches fixos" — útil para músicos que transpoem cantando ou acompanhando de ouvido.
