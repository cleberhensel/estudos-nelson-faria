# [SRC-042]: How to Transpose Guitar Chords With a Capo — Number System Shortcut

**URL**: https://playguitar.com/how-to-transpose-chords/
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / guitar tool + theory

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "Go DOWN the chromatic scale from your target key until you hit a playing key you like — C, A, G, E, or D. These are the keys with easy open chord shapes. Count the semitones between those two points. Capo UP that many frets and play your shapes."

> "Instead of thinking in letter names, you can assign each chord a number based on its position in the scale. In any major key, the chords follow this pattern: I | ii | iii | IV | V | vi — Major | minor | minor | Major | Major | minor."

> "So the key of F becomes: F – Gm – Am – Bb – C – Dm (Or just: 1 – 2 – 3 – 4 – 5 – 6). And the key of D becomes: D – Em – F#m – G – A – Bm (Same numbers: 1 – 2 – 3 – 4 – 5 – 6)."

> "Once you see the pattern, transposing becomes instant. You're not converting F to D to G — you're just moving the same relationships to a new starting point."

> "This is the foundation of how professional musicians think about music."

## Primary claims

- Capo: contar semitons entre tom-alvo (sounding) e tom das formas (playing key).
- Sistema numérico (I–vi) unifica capo + transposição: mesmas relações, novo ponto de partida.
- Tabela 1–6 idêntica em F e D demonstra independência de letras.
- Atalho profissional: pensar relações, não converter acorde a acorde.
- Integração capo + números evita confusão entre shapes e tom real.

## Relevance to charter Q3

Sintetiza capo logic + number thinking num único workflow — diretamente aplicável a violonistas de cifra que usam capotraste para mudar tom sem re-aprender progressão.
