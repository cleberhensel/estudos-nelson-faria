## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "You're not memorizing chords anymore — you're memorizing the shape of a song. The 1 is always home. The 4 always feels like that lift. The 5 always wants to resolve back. Once you know a song as 1 – 4 – 5 – 6m, the key doesn't matter."

> "Now when the key changes, I don't even have to think. I already know the numbers. I know what chord comes next in my mind — I just figure out what that chord is in the new key, and I'm there. No paper needed."

> "The guitarist plays in G with a capo, the keys player is in Bb, but they're both reading the same numbers."

> "The Nashville Number System (NNS) is a method of transcribing music by representing chords as scale degree numbers instead of chord names. In any key, the root chord is 1, the fourth is 4, the fifth is 5, and so on. This makes charts key-independent — musicians can read the same chart in any key without rewriting it."

> "Minor chords keep the same number but add a lowercase 'm' (or minus sign). So Am in the key of G becomes 2m. Em becomes 6m."

## Primary claims

- Internalizar progressão como números elimina reescrita de charts e pânico em mudanças de tom.
- Equipe multi-instrumento pode compartilhar um chart numérico em tons diferentes (violão+capo vs teclado).
- Qualidade do acorde (m, 7, etc.) modifica o numeral, não o grau.
- Ferramenta online converte cifra ↔ NNS bidirecionalmente.
- Pensamento funcional (1=home, 4=lift, 5=resolve) acelera transposição mental.

## Relevance to charter Q3

Testemunho prático de transposição instantânea por números em contexto de palco; valida NNS como alternativa operacional à cifra por letras para músicos de acompanhamento.
