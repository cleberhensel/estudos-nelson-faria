## Citações / trechos

> "The key to learning ear training is to exercise your inner hearing. It's like isolating a muscle: if you 'cheat' and use an easier technique, you won't really get stronger. In ear training, that means using only a reference Do, and extrapolating all the other sounds from inner hearing."

> "If you play what you're trying to hear and sing, you switch to the echo memory, a short-term memory part of the brain that babies use to imitate words as they learn how to communicate. We sometimes refer to this kind of instant copying or echoing as 'parroting.' It doesn't take skill or mental power."

> "If you play an instrument in ear training, always play after you've sung. When you've done your best to find the note with your inner hearing and voice, you can play to check yourself."

> "Ear Training 1 is the first of a series of four required courses... Through guided practice of ear training techniques, your musical perception and performance will become more accurate and you will become more fluent in translating sound into notation and notation into sound."

---

## Claims extraídos

1. **Regra pedagógica central**: cantar/audiar ANTES de tocar; tocar só para verificar.
2. "Parroting" (copiar do instrumento) é considerado anti-padrão — ativa memória de eco, não audiation.
3. Movable-do com **referência Do única** — extrapolar demais notas por ouvido interno.
4. Técnicas listadas: solfège móvel, condução, sistemas de contagem, ditado.
5. Skills adquiridos aplicam-se a "composition, arranging, production, learning new repertoire, and bandleading; improvisation and musical interaction in performance."

---

## Relevância para Q6

- Audiation permite ao acompanhante **ouvir a melodia vocal internamente** e inferir função tonal antes de escolher voicings.
- Cantar a linha melódica do cantor (mesmo mentalmente) ajuda a detectar notas extremas e necessidade de transposição.

---

## Relevância para Q7

- **Alta**: princípio pedagógico explícito e contra-intuitivo para guitarristas (que tendem a "caçar" notas no braço primeiro).
- Metodologia replicável em exercícios diários: Do de referência → cantar intervalo → verificar no instrumento.
- Fundamenta abordagem funcional (notas por função na tonalidade, não por nome absoluto).
