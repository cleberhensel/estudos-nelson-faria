# [SRC-048]: Four-Chord Schemas — Open Music Theory

**URL**: https://pressbooks.nebraska.edu/openmusictheory/chapter/4-chord-schemas/
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: textbook / music theory

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "I, IV, V, and vi are the most common harmonies in pop music, and they can be arranged into several schemas, each with a distinct sound."

> "The doo-wop schema is I–vi–IV–V, and it was common in 1960s pop music."

> "The singer/songwriter schema may be the best known of all the four-chord schemas. A common Roman numeral analysis for this schema is vi–IV–I–V, or Ami–F–C–G in C major, but the rotation starting with I is also exceedingly common."

> "In recent years (since about 2010), another type of four-chord schema has become increasingly common: IV–V–vi–I, or VI–VII–i–III in minor. We will refer to this as the hopscotch schema because of its root motion: step, step, skip."

> "In the doo-wop schema, the tonic is approached with very traditional authentic motion. In the singer/songwriter schema, the major tonic is approached with plagal motion. In the hopscotch schema, the major tonic is approached by a skip."

## Primary claims

- Quatro acordes (I, IV, V, vi) geram múltiplos "schemas" reconhecíveis pela ordem e tipo de cadência.
- Schemas nomeados: doo-wop (I–vi–IV–V), singer/songwriter (vi–IV–I–V ou I–V–vi–IV), hopscotch (IV–V–vi–I, pós-2010).
- Rotação do mesmo conjunto de acordes muda a sensação (autêntica vs. plágio vs. salto).
- Internalização por schema (não por acordes isolados) acelera reconhecimento auditivo.
- Hopscotch representa evolução estatística recente do pop.

## Relevance to charter Q4

Framework pedagógico para categorizar e memorizar progressões dominantes. Músico que internaliza schemas reconhece loops inteiros (não acordes soltos) — alinhado à pergunta "como internalizá-los". Base teórica para ear training de progressões pop.
