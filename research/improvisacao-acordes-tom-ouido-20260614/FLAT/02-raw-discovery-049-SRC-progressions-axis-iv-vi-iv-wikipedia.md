## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "The I–V–vi–IV chord progression or Axis progression is a common chord progression popular across several music genres. It uses the I, V, vi, and IV chords of the diatonic scale."

> "A 2008 medley by the comedy group the Axis of Awesome, called 'Four Chords', demonstrated the ubiquity of the progression in popular music, for comic effect."

> "It was originally written in D major (thus the progression being D major, A major, B minor, G major) and performed live in the key of E major."

> "As of May 2020, the two most popular versions have been viewed over 100 million times combined."

> "Rotations include vi–IV–I–V (i.e. the 'pessimistic' inversion)."

## Primary claims

- I–V–vi–IV é progressão transversal a múltiplos gêneros (pop, rock, reggae, country).
- Axis of Awesome (2008) popularizou culturalmente a ideia de "quatro acordes para milhares de hits".
- Rotações (vi–IV–I–V) são a mesma progressão com ponto de entrada diferente.
- Transponível a qualquer tom via graus romanos — chave para acompanhamento espontâneo.
- Variações com bVII (Mixolydian) e subtonic existem mas são subconjunto.
