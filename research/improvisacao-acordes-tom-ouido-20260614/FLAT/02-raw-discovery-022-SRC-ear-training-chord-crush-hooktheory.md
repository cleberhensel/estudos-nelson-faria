# [SRC-022]: Chord Crush — Real-Song Chord Progression Ear Training (Hooktheory)
**URL**: https://www.hooktheory.com/chord-crush
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: Interactive app / gamified ear training

## Verbatim snippets / quotes
> "Chord Crush teaches you to recognize chords by solving fun, interactive puzzles of increasing difficulty."

> "Conventional ear training courses attempt to cover a bunch of stuff that quite frankly, most of you are not interested in. Being able to tell the difference between diminished 7th and minor 6th intervals, or knowing whether an augmented 6th chord is 'Italian' or 'German' is great if you teach classical music. But for most of the rest of us, we just want to hear a song and know how it goes on a guitar or piano."

> "You will be presented with a chord progression pulled from a real song from our Theorytab Database with some of the chords missing. Drag a chord from the available options below to fill in the missing ones!"

> "Chord Crush is based on a relative numbered system for identifying chords as they relate to a key. The goal is to identify chords in this relative system and to hear the numbers rather than particular names. So for example, you will learn to hear a progression as one → four → five not C → F → G."

## Primary claims
- Pop-oriented musicians benefit more from real-song progression puzzles than classical interval/chord nomenclature drills.
- Adaptive difficulty via rating system targets puzzles at current hearing ability.
- Relative-number (Roman functional) identification is the core skill, not absolute pitch naming.
- Missing-chord dictation from real recordings bridges isolated chord ID to musical context.
- Complex pop/jazz embellishments remain in advanced levels for experienced hearers.

## Relevance to charter Q2
Validates Q2 emphasis on progression dictation over abstract classical drills; relative-number hearing (I, IV, V, vi) transfers across tom and supports repertório transcription without perfect pitch.
