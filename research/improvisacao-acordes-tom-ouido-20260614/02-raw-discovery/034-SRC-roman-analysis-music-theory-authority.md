**Type**: article / music theory reference

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "Roman numeral analysis is the standard framework used in Western music theory to identify and label chords according to their function within a key, rather than by fixed pitch names."

> "Understanding this method unlocks the ability to recognize harmonic patterns across styles, instruments, and transpositions — a foundational skill covered in depth across Music Theory Authority."

> "Borrowed chords from the parallel minor are common in rock and pop. In a major-key context, a ♭VII chord (e.g., B♭ major in C major) is borrowed from C minor. Roman numeral notation makes this modal mixture immediately legible."

> "The I–V–vi–IV progression underlies hundreds of pop songs and is immediately transferable across keys through Roman numeral notation alone — a direct demonstration of the system's transposition-neutral utility."

## Primary claims

- Graus romanos identificam função dentro da tonalidade, não pitch absoluto.
- Maiúsculas = acordes maiores; minúsculas = menores/diminutos; símbolos extras para empréstimos modais (♭VII, etc.).
- Processo em 7 passos aplicável a qualquer instrumento ou gênero tonal.
- Notação romana torna empréstimos harmônicos (modal mixture) legíveis na transposição.
- I–V–vi–IV exemplifica utilidade "transposition-neutral" do sistema.
