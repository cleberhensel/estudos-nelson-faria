# [SRC-021]: Recognizing Chord Progressions by Ear — Guitar-Oriented Method
**URL**: https://guitarwiz.app/articles/recognize-chord-progressions-by-ear/
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: Practical tutorial / instrument-specific pedagogy

## Verbatim snippets / quotes
> "The single best entry point for recognizing chord progressions is the bass note. In most popular music, the lowest note you hear tells you the root of the chord."

> "Practice this: play a C major chord, then a C minor chord. Go back and forth. Listen to the difference. That feeling is what you're training your ear to detect in recordings."

> "Open the chord library and explore chords in a single key. Play through each chord in the key of C major (C, Dm, Em, F, G, Am, Bdim) and listen to how each one sounds relative to the others."

> "Use the Song Maker to build common progressions like I - V - vi - IV or ii - V - I. Play them in different keys and listen for the pattern rather than the specific pitches."

> "Try building a progression you heard in a song using the chord progression builder. Start with the bass notes you identified, then experiment with chord qualities until it matches what you heard."

## Primary claims
- Bass-note/root identification precedes quality identification in progression dictation.
- Major vs. minor quality is trained through direct A/B comparison on same root.
- Functional Roman-numeral patterns (I-V-vi-IV, ii-V-I) should be recognized across keys, not as fixed letter names.
- Scale-degree listening within one key builds relative harmonic memory.
- Transcription workflow: key → bass roots → quality → verify by playing along.
