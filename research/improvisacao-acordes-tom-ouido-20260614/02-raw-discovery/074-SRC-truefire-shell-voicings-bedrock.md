## Citações relevantes

> "Clarity: Jazz harmony can get pretty dense and often moves quickly. Minimalist voicings cut through the mix without ever sounding muddy."

> "Space: In a jazz group, the guitarist's job is to support, not to dominate. Leaving sonic real estate for the bassist, pianist, and soloist is crucial."

> "Movement: At its heart, jazz is about the smooth melodic movement between chords. We call this voice leading. Efficient, streamlined voicings make that elegant motion possible."

> "Think of poor voice leading as jumping randomly all over the fretboard. Good voice leading is like walking gracefully from one room to another. It prioritizes minimal movement, often by keeping notes in common between chords or moving the other notes by the smallest possible step."

> "Master Your Shell Voicings: Start by playing shell voicings (Root-3rd-7th) through a II-V-I progression in all 12 keys. Focus only on moving smoothly between the guide tones (the 3rds and 7ths) of each chord. This is the absolute bedrock of good voice leading."

---

## Claims extraídos

1. Três razões para voicings minimalistas: **clareza**, **espaço**, **movimento** (voice leading).
2. Papel do guitarrista = **suporte** — não dominar o mix (relevante com cantor).
3. Shell voicings em **II-V-I × 12 keys** = exercício fundacional absoluto.
4. Foco exclusivo em guide tones (3ª/7ª) ao praticar transição entre acordes.
5. Voicings eficientes permitem comping interativo em ensemble.
