# [SRC-036]: How Chord Progressions, Roman Numerals and Songwriting Intersect

**URL**: https://www.secretsofsongwriting.com/2011/07/15/how-chord-progressions-roman-numerals-and-songwriting-intersect/
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / songwriting pedagogy (Gary Ewer)

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "R.Ns do more than simply tell us which chords are which. They help us accomplish two things: transposing music, and identifying chord function."

> "If a progression is: C Dm G Am, the Roman numerals will be I ii V vi. To transpose, you simply build new chords by finding the chord roots represented by the R.N. in a new key. So if you're transposing to E major, instead of transposing everything up a major 3rd, you simply build chords on the 1st, 2nd, 5th and 6th notes of the new key of E major, giving us: E F#m B C#m."

> "Roman numerals allow you to instantly compare one song's progression to a different song in a different key. By using R.N., you can tell that C Dm G Am is, on a theoretical level, identical to E F#m B C#m."

> "It's a worthwhile exercise to convert pop song progressions to Roman numerals, because it allows you to do a level of musical analysis that's difficult to do in any other way."

## Primary claims

- Graus romanos servem duplamente: transposição + identificação de função (tônica, dominante, etc.).
- Transpor ≠ contar semitons cegamente; = reconstruir acordes nos mesmos graus da nova tonalidade.
- Progressões idênticas em tons diferentes tornam-se comparáveis instantaneamente via R.N.
- Exercício recomendado: converter hits pop para numerais romanos.
- Método aplicável ao universo da música popular ocidental, não só erudita.

## Relevance to charter Q3

Pedagogia clara de transposição por grau (alternativa à contagem cromática de cifras), alinhada ao objetivo de "tocar em qualquer tom sem re-aprender".
