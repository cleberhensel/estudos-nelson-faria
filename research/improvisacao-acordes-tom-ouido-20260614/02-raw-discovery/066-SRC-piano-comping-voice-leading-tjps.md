## Citações relevantes

> "Comping means 'accompanying' or 'complementing' a soloist by playing the chords. While comping, you have two main objectives: [1] complement the soloist [2] stay out of the soloist's way."

> "Law of Conservation of Energy (Voice Leading): Minimise movement between chords (this creates smooth voice leading). Have as many common tones as possible. Move the notes that do change as little as possible."

> "Soprano Voice Leading: Create a counter-melody with the top note of your chords. The soprano voice (top note) should move smoothly and melodically and largely in steps."

> "Comping is not about just playing the chords perfectly. You really have to listen to your soloist and actually complement them. No two solos are the same, therefore no two comps should be the same."

> "If the solo is busy – comp sparingly. If there's a pause in the solo (breath) – comp busily."

---

## Claims extraídos

1. **Dois objetivos** de comping: complementar + não atrapalhar (registro, densidade, silêncio).
2. **Lei da conservação de energia**: common tones + movimento mínimo = voice leading eficaz.
3. **Soprano voice leading**: nota superior do voicing forma contramelodia — movimento conjunto preferível.
4. Comping é **improvisado e reativo** — não repetitivo; densidade inversa à atividade do soloista.
5. Técnicas avançadas citadas: approach chords, side-slipping, chromatic movements, parallelism.

## Relevância para Q5

**Alta.** Framework completo para acompanhamento piano em tempo real: voicings + voice leading + regras rítmicas adaptativas. Aplicável a contexto de acompanhamento vocal (soloist = cantor).

## Relevância para Q6

**Alta.** "Stay out of the soloist's way" implica gestão de registro — não competir com tessitura vocal. Soprano voice leading permite escolher voicings cuja nota superior complemente (não colida com) a melodia cantada. Comping esparso quando voz está ativa.
