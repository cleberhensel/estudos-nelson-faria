## Verbatim snippets / quotes
> Develop practical skills for identifying chord progressions through focused listening and transcription in this undergraduate ear training course. Using solfège, voice-leading concepts, and real musical examples, you'll practice hearing harmony both vertically and horizontally to recognize diatonic, chromatic, and functional progressions with confidence.

> Identify chord progressions by ear using a clear, step-by-step listening and transcription process.

> Recognize diatonic triads and 7th chords, non-diatonic chords, and secondary dominants by ear in musical contexts.

> Hear harmony both vertically and horizontally by tracking voice leading, guide tones, and chord inversions.

> _Harmonic Ear Training: Recognizing Chord Progressions_ details a step-by-step "vertical" (from the root up) and "horizontal" (using the song's key as a reference point) approach to hearing chord changes and progressions.

## Primary claims (one-liners)
- Berklee teaches dual vertical (chord-quality) and horizontal (key-relative solfège) listening for progression ID.
- Secondary dominants and chromatic chords are explicit ear-training targets alongside diatonic function.
- Guide tones and voice-leading connect functional theory to real-time recognition while performing.
- Course applies directly to improvising, arranging, and transcribing—not analysis-only.
