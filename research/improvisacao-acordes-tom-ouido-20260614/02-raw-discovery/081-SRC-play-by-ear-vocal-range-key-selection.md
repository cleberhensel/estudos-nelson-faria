## Citações / trechos

> "The first thing to do is to find the lowest and highest notes you can sing comfortably... You should also think about the range in which your voice sounds the best." — iReal Pro (Massimo Biolcati)

> "Find the lowest and highest notes of the song and see if they fit in your vocal range. If they don't, count how many semitones you need to move up or down." — iReal Pro

> "When you get to these parts choose a musical place to switch the octave of the melody so it moves into a comfortable range for you. This will come very handy during jam sessions or other situations where changing the key of the song is not possible." — iReal Pro

> "Experiment singing the song in at least 3 different keys before making a final decision. This provides contrast, making the key that is most suitable for your voice more evident." — Throga

> "The note you can hit once isn't the note you should build a performance around. What matters is your comfortable range." — MyKaraoke

> "Singers frequently need adjustments of 1 to 5 whole steps, or 2 to 10 semitones, to fit a song to their range." — MyKaraoke (citando 30 Day Singer)

## Claims extraídos

1. **Range confortável ≠ range extremo** — tom deve caber em frases completas, não notas isoladas.
2. Procedimento: mapear range vocal → mapear range da melodia → calcular semitons de transposição.
3. Testar **mínimo 3 tons** antes de decidir — contraste perceptivo.
4. Mudança de oitava em trechos específicos quando range da música > range do cantor (ex.: Billie Holiday).
5. Ajustes típicos: 2–10 semitons; covers M→F frequentemente +5 semitons ou mais.
6. Tom da música coincide com tom da introdução ~69% das vezes (Throga) — heurística útil mas não infalível.
