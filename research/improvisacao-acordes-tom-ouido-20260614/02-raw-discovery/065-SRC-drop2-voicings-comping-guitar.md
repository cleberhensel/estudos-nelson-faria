## Citações relevantes

> "These four-note chord shapes built on the middle strings are the primary tool of jazz comping. They contain the essential chord tones (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th), they're fully moveable up and down the neck, and they're designed for voice leading."

> "The real power of drop 2 is how smoothly the shapes connect through a ii-V-I progression. The guide tones (3rd and 7th of each chord) move by half step or stay on the same note."

> "Play the bottom two notes of the drop 2 voicing as a bass note on the beat, followed by the top two notes as an off-beat chord hit. This creates a stride-piano-like accompaniment on guitar."

> "Drop 2 voicings are the building block of jazz guitar harmony. Compact, moveable, and voice-leading-friendly, they give you a complete comping vocabulary with just three chord types (major 7th, minor 7th, dominant 7th) across two string groups."

## Claims extraídos

1. **Drop 2** = ferramenta primária de comping jazz na guitarra (4 notas, cordas médias).
2. Totalmente **transponível** (sem cordas abertas) — ideal para mudança de tom rápida.
3. ii-V-I: guide tones movem por semitom ou common tone — voice leading "exemplar".
4. **Padrão stride na guitarra**: baixo (2 notas inferiores) no tempo + acorde (2 superiores) no contratempo.
5. Domínio = 3 qualidades × 2 string groups = vocabulário completo de comping.

---

## Relevância para Q5

**Alta.** Evolução natural de shells para comping de 4 notas; padrão rítmico stride integra baixo melódico + acorde. Essencial para arranjo em tempo real em contexto jazz/bossa.

## Relevância para Q6

**Média.** Voicings em registro médio-alto (cordas 2-5) posicionam harmonia acima de voz de barítono/contralto; transponibilidade total facilita ajuste de tom ao cantor sem re-aprender shapes.
