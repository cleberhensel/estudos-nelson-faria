## Citações relevantes

> "A tritone substitution is a jazz harmonic technique where a dominant chord (V7) is replaced by another dominant chord located a tritone interval away. This substitution maintains the tension and resolution characteristic of the original chord but adds a unique, chromatic twist to the progression."

> "In a jazz ensemble context, where piano or guitar players often use rootless voicings, the bass player actually has significant control. They can decide to change a V chord to its tritone substitute simply by changing the bass note. The piano or guitar players don't even have to change the chords they are playing."

> "Piano and guitar players often play rootless voicings, which omit the root note of a chord, focusing instead on the 3rd, 7th, and other extensions or alterations."

> "The ii-V-I progression, foundational in jazz, transforms into a compelling chromatic bass movement by substituting the V for its tritone."

> "The best time to use a tritone substitution is typically in a dominant chord that resolves down a perfect fifth. It's particularly effective in ii-V-I progressions, turnarounds, and places where you want to introduce harmonic variety."

## Claims extraídos

1. **Tritone sub**: V7 substituído por dom7 a trítono de distância — mesmos guide tones (3ª e 7ª trocadas).
2. **Spontaneidade**: baixista muda apenas a nota de baixo; harmonia upper-structure (rootless voicings) pode permanecer.
3. Rootless voicings (3ª+7ª+extensões) são padrão quando baixo cobre root — modelo piano/guitarra + baixo.
4. ii-V-I com tritone sub gera **movimento cromático de baixo** — voice leading no registro grave.
5. Aplicação: dominantes que resolvem em 5ª perfeita, turnarounds, busca de variedade harmônica.
