## Citações / trechos

> "A fantastic way to start is by locking onto the bass line. When you're learning a song by ear, the bass player is your new best friend. Why? Because they almost always play the root note of the chord right on the downbeat." — TrueFire

> "Start With Vocal Lines, Not Guitar Parts... Vocal lines are far simpler, clearer, and easier to pick out because they stand out prominently in a mix." — Breakthrough Guitar

> "Loop a Small Section: Isolate just two or three seconds of the song... Slow it Down: Knock the speed down to 75% or even 50%." — TrueFire

> "Rather than listening for chords, harmonies, and melodies all at the same time, try an exercise where you listen and repeat only the rhythms." — Acoustic Guitar

> "Intervals are the building blocks of melodies and chords... train your ear to recognize intervals across different chord types and musical settings." — Reading School of Guitar

## Claims extraídos

1. **Sequência profissional recomendada**: melodia vocal → linha de baixo (root notes) → acordes → detalhes.
2. Ferramentas de loop e slow-down (sem mudar pitch) são padrão de mercado.
3. Separação de variáveis: ritmo isolado, depois pitch isolado — reduz carga cognitiva.
4. Prática diária curta (15 min) ou 3×/semana (30–45 min) — volume > maratonas esporádicas.
5. Intervalos devem ser treinados em **contexto harmônico variado**, não só via "música de referência" fixa.
6. Trial-and-error em uma corda (ex.: "Twinkle Twinkle") como porta de entrada.
