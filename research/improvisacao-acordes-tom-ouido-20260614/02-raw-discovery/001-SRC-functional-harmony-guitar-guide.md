# [SRC-001]: A Guitarist's Guide to Functional Harmony

**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / pedagogical blog

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "Functional harmony is a way of understanding how chords behave within a key. It explains why one chord feels like home, why another creates tension, and how those movements shape the emotional flow of a song."

> "Most songs built around a tonal center rely on three core harmonic functions: tonic, subdominant, and dominant."
> - Tonic (I, vi): Feels stable. This is home base.
> - Subdominant (ii, IV): Creates motion or lift. It gently pulls away from the tonic.
> - Dominant (V, vii°): Builds tension. It leads back to the tonic and creates a sense of resolution.

> "A secondary dominant is a dominant seventh chord that temporarily targets a chord other than the tonic."

## Primary claims

- Acordes em tonalidade têm funções previsíveis (T, SD, D) que permitem antecipar movimentos.
- vi funciona como variante tônica; ii e IV como subdominantes; V e vii° como dominantes.
- Dominantes secundárias apontam para acordes diatônicos fora da função imediata, criando tensão breve.
- Progressões clássicas (I–IV–V–I, I–vi–IV–V) exemplificam arco emocional funcional.

## Relevance to charter Q1

Fundamento central: prever acordes = reconhecer função, não decorar letras.
