# [SRC-032]: The Nashville Number System Explained for Guitar

**Type**: article / guitar theory lesson

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "The Nashville Number System is a way of writing chord progressions using numbers instead of chord names. Each number represents a chord built from a scale degree."

> "For example, the progression 1 – 5 – 6 – 4: In C major: C – G – Am – F. In G major: G – D – Em – C. In A major: A – E – F#m – D. The pattern stays the same. Only the key changes."

> "The Nashville Number System always comes from the major scale, even when you are working in a minor key."

> "With numbers, it becomes simple. For example: 1 – 5 – 6 – 4. In C major: C – G – Am – F. In D major: D – A – Bm – G. The numbers stay the same. Only the key changes."

> "Everything in the Nashville Number System starts with the 1 chord. On guitar, this means you need to be able to quickly find your root note anywhere on the fretboard."

## Primary claims

- NNS mapeia cada acorde ao grau da escala maior, independente do instrumento.
- Transposição = manter números, trocar apenas a tonalidade de referência.
- Mesmo em contexto menor, a referência teórica permanece a escala maior.
- Fluência no violão exige localizar rapidamente a raiz do acorde 1 em qualquer região do braço.
- Benefícios: transpor instantaneamente, reconhecer padrões entre músicas, comunicação entre músicos.
