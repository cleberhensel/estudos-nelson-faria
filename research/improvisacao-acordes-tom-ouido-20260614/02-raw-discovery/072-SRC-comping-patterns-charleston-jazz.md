## Citações relevantes

> "Comping is a jazz technique used by the rhythm section of a jazz ensemble to carry the chord changes, apply rhythmic style, and complement other musicians playing melodically."

> "There are common rhythmic comping patterns you should learn to get yourself going. These provide you several frameworks you can use to guide your playing. You never want to be robotic and repeat these blindly — follow your ear using these as a guide."

> "The first rhythms you should learn are the Charleston and Reverse Charleston. These are simple syncopated rhythms inspired by the 1920's style of dance with the same name."

> "A 2 feel emphasizes half-notes — feeling 4 beats as 2. Similarly a 4 feel emphasizes all 4 beats by emphasizing the quarter notes."

> "Anticipate upcoming chords. You don't have to wait until the downbeat to change chords."

---

## Claims extraídos

1. **Comping** = carregar mudanças de acorde + estilo rítmico + complementar melodia (voz/solo).
2. Padrões base: **Charleston** (beat 1 + & de 2), **Reverse Charleston** (& de 1 + beat 3), **Freddie Green** (4 quarter notes).
3. **2 feel** vs **4 feel**: densidade rítmica adaptável ao contexto (balada vs swing).
4. Padrões são **frameworks**, não fórmulas fixas — escuta e reação ao soloista prevalecem.
5. **Antecipação** de acordes cria forward motion — mudar acorde antes do downbeat.
6. Guitarra: começar com drop 2 e drop 3 voicings para comping.

---

## Relevância para Q5

**Alta.** Preenchimento da dimensão rítmica de Q5 — voicings (SRC-061–065) + padrões rítmicos = acompanhamento completo. Essencial para bossa/samba (Charleston = esqueleto rítmico).

---

## Relevância para Q6

**Média.** Comping esparso (2 feel, silêncio) deixa espaço para voz; densidade rítmica deve complementar (não competir com) fraseado vocal. Antecipação de acordes acompanha rubato do cantor.
