## Verbatim snippets / quotes
> "Hearing Chord Progressions is one of the core courses of the Bruce Arnold System."

> "We call 'Hearing Chord Progressions' a dual course because it develops the ability to hear tertial based chord changes while simultaneously improving instrumental technique."

> "If we have a chord progression you will either hear the whole progression in one key or parts of the progression in one key. This will depend on how quickly the chords are moving one to another and the tempo of the tune. Usually the longer you stay on a chord the better chance you will have of hearing that chord in its own key center."

> "Now if you listen again to the notes of the F7 chord as you are hearing it in the key of C, you will not hear: F=1, A=3, C=5, Eb=b7 you will hear: F = 4, A = 6, C= 1, Eb = b3."

> "Études covering all 13 seventh chords through all possible harmonic cycles. 1109 pages of exercises!"

## Primary claims
- Chord-progression hearing requires prior key-note recognition; secondary dominants are a critical bridge skill.
- Chords heard in a progression are perceived by scale-degree function within the prevailing key center, not as isolated root-stack intervals.
- The course covers 13 tertial seventh-chord types through all harmonic cycles and 96 permutations.
- MetroDrone/tuba drone tools integrate time-feel with harmonic hearing.
- Fast-moving progressions may be heard as multiple local key centers; tempo and chord duration affect perceptual strategy.

## Relevance to charter Q2
Essential for Q2 chord-progression dictation: teaches hearing V7, maj7, min7 etc. as functional colors (e.g., secondary dominant as 4-6-1-b3 in key of C) rather than letter-name stacks — directly applicable to Brazilian/pop progressions and tom-by-ear work.
