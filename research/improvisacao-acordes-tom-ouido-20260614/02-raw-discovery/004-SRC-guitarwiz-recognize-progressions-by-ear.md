# [SRC-004]: How to Recognize Chord Progressions in Any Song by Ear

**URL**: https://guitarwiz.app/articles/recognize-chord-progressions-by-ear/
**Captured**: 2026-06-14
**Type**: article / practical guide

## Verbatim snippets / quotes

> "The single best entry point for recognizing chord progressions is the bass note. In most popular music, the lowest note you hear tells you the root of the chord."

> "Instead of thinking in specific chord names, learn to think in numbers (Roman numerals). The I chord is home. The V chord creates tension that wants to resolve back to I."

> "Descending bass (moving down step by step) often signals a line cliche or a descending progression."

> "Ascending bass by fourths (going up a fourth) signals movement around the circle of fifths."

## Primary claims

- Baixo = raiz na maioria da música popular → ponto de entrada para ouvido.
- Pensar em graus romanos desacopla percepção do tom absoluto.
- Padrões de movimento de baixo (descendente, quartas ascendentes) são assinaturas reconhecíveis.
- Treinar cada grau diatônico relativamente constrói mapa auditivo interno.

## Relevance to charter Q1, Q2, Q5

Protocolo prático para músicos de cifra: baixo → função → acorde.
